About Me

My name is Elizabeth Gonzalez, and I am a third-year Ph.D. candidate in Astronomy and Astrophysics at Penn State. My research focuses on developing methods to mitigate stellar variability in extreme precision radial velocity measurements, with the goal of improving our ability to detect and characterize exoplanets. I am particularly interested in the ways stellar surface phenomena—such as convection and magnetic activity—impact high-resolution spectra and limit our sensitivity to low-mass planets. My work combines observational data and modeling to better understand these effects and to develop tools that can separate stellar signals from planetary ones. I am a member of the NEID and HPF instrument teams, where I work with high-precision spectroscopic data to advance these efforts.
